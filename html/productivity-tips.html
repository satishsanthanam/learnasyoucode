# Technique Description
1 2-minute rule If a task takes less than 2 minutes to complete, do it immediately to avoid touching it twice.
2 5-second rule To avoid procrastination, count backward from 5 to 1 and take action immediately.
3 Rule of 72 Avoid making decisions when hungry, angry, tired, or lonely; wait 72 hours to prevent unnecessary stress.
4 Daily highlight Choose one main task each day to prioritize and commit to completing.
5 Ask who not how Delegate tasks by asking who can do it instead of how you can do it, saving time and leveraging others' expertise.
6 90-1-90 rule Spend the first 90 minutes of your day on one task for 90 days to achieve your goals.
7 12-week year rule Set 12-week goals instead of annual goals to create urgency and improve execution.
8 Biological Prime Time Identify the time of day when you are most energized and productive, and do your most important tasks then.
9 80% rule Get feedback at 80% completion to avoid unnecessary perfectionism and focus on what's needed.
10 Eat the Frog first Tackle the most significant and challenging task first thing in the morning.
11 Parkinson's law Work expands to fill the time available for its completion; set shorter deadlines to increase productivity.
12 80/20 rule 80% of your results come from 20% of your activities; focus on the most impactful tasks.
13 ABCDE metdod Prioritize tasks from most important (A) to least important (E) and tackle them in that order.
14 Time auditing Keep a detailed record of daily activities to understand time usage and improve efficiency.
15 Don't break the chain Mark each day you complete a task or habit on a calendar to build consistency.
16 Energy management Maintain high energy by getting enough sleep, exercising, and taking time off from work.
17 Pomodoro Technique Work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break; repeat this cycle four times, then take a 30-minute break.
18 Batch processing Group similar tasks and do them together to reduce startup time and increase focus.
19 Salami slice method Break down overwhelming tasks into smaller slices and commit to working on one slice at a time.
20 Outcome visualization Spend time visualizing the desired outcome of your goals to maintain motivation and clarity.
21 Warren Buffett strategy (25-5 rule) Write down your top 25 career goals, circle the top 5, and focus only on them, avoiding the remaining 20.
22 Feynman technique Enhance understanding by studying as if you have to teach the concepts to someone else.
23 Five whys technique Ask "why" five times to get to the root cause of a problem and prevent recurring issues.
24 Habit stacking Develop new habits by stacking them onto existing habits.
25 Rest and sleep Adequate rest and sleep significantly improve brain function and productivity.
26 Quality time over quantity time Focus on the quality of time spent with family and relationships rather than the total hours.
27 Say no often Every yes to one thing is a no to another; prioritize commitments to avoid overcommitment.
28 Working blocks Divide your day into three parts: strategic block, buffer block, and breakout block for rest and recovery.
29 theming your days Focus on one activity each day, such as administrative tasks on Mondays or client meetings on Fridays.
30 Decision fatigue Minimize daily decisions like what to wear or eat to save mental energy for more important tasks.
31 Power of routine Establish a consistent daily routine to build good habits and reduce the need for willpower.
32 Digital detox Periodically disconnect from digital devices to reduce distractions and mental clutter.
33 Respect your future self Make choices that your future self will thank you for, ensuring they align with long-term goals.
34 Spotlight effect Understand that people are not constantly judging you; focus more on your tasks and less on others' opinions.
35 Ulysses pact Make present choices that force future actions, like setting up automatic savings or deleting distracting apps.
36 Plan your weeks Plan your 168 hours before the week begins to ensure alignment witd long-term goals and priorities.
37 Always carry a notebook Capture spontaneous ideas and solutions that come from your subconscious mind. (I use https://keep.google.com)
