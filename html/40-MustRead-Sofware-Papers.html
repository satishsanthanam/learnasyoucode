Credits: 40 Must read Software Papers

 

# Paper Name Author Summary Link
1 Google File System Sanjay Ghemawat, Howard Gobioff, Shun-Tak Leung This foundational paper describes the Google File System (GFS), a scalable distributed file system for large distributed data-intensive applications. [https://research.google/pubs/pub51/ Link]
2 MapReduce: Simplified Data Processing on Large Clusters Jeffrey Dean, Sanjay Ghemawat This white paper is an essential read on the MapReduce programming model that enables processing vast amounts of data across many machines. [https://research.google/pubs/pub62/ Link]
3 Dynamo: Amazon's Highly Available Key-Value Store Giuseppe DeCandia et al. In this research paper from AWS, you will learn about Dynamo, Amazon's key-value store designed for high availability and scalability, used to manage the state of various services. [https://allthingsdistributed.com/files/amazon-dynamo-sosp2007.pdf Link]
4 Bigtable: A Distributed Storage System for Structured Data Fay Chang et al. This paper details Bigtable, Google's distributed storage system for managing structured data designed to scale to a very large size. [https://research.google/pubs/pub27898/ Link]
5 The Chubby Lock Service for Loosely-Coupled Distributed Systems Mike Burrows This paper presents Chubby, a lock service for loosely-coupled distributed systems designed to manage coarse-grained locks. [https://research.google/pubs/pub27897/ Link]
6 Paxos Made Simple Leslie Lamport A simplified explanation of the Paxos consensus algorithm, which is foundational for understanding distributed systems and achieving consensus. [https://research.microsoft.com/en-us/um/people/lamport/pubs/paxos-simple.pdf Link]
7 Raft Consensus Algorithm Diego Ongaro, John Ousterhout An approachable and understandable consensus algorithm designed as an alternative to Paxos, providing better understandability and manageability. [https://ramcloud.stanford.edu/raft.pdf Link]
8 Spanner: Google's Globally-Distributed Database James C. Corbett et al. This paper introduces Spanner, Google's scalable, multi-version, globally-distributed, and synchronously-replicated database. [https://research.google/pubs/pub39966/ Link]
9 The Log-Structured Merge-Tree (LSM-Tree) Patrick O'Neil et al. The LSM-Tree paper introduces a method for improving write performance in databases, which is crucial for high-write systems. [https://cs.umb.edu/~poneil/lsmtree.pdf Link]
10 Kafka: A Distributed Messaging System for Log Processing Jay Kreps et al. This paper describes Kafka, a distributed messaging system that is highly scalable and fault-tolerant, widely used for real-time data pipelines. [https://storageconference.org/2011/Papers/19.Kreps.pdf Link]
11 Cassandra - A Decentralized Structured Storage System Avinash Lakshman, Prashant Malik This paper introduces Cassandra, a decentralized storage system designed to handle large amounts of data across many commodity servers. [https://www.cs.cornell.edu/projects/ladis2009/papers/lakshman-ladis2009.pdf Link]
12 Mesos: A Platform for Fine-Grained Resource Sharing in the Data Center Benjamin Hindman et al. Learn about Apache Mesos, a resource management platform that allows multiple distributed systems to efficiently share cluster resources. [https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~alig/papers/mesos.pdf Link]
13 The CAP Theorem Eric Brewer This white paper introduces the CAP Theorem, which states that it is impossible for a distributed data store to simultaneously provide consistency, availability, and partition tolerance. [https://www.cs.cornell.edu/courses/cs7412/2013sp/papers/decisive.pdf Link]
14 The Tail at Scale Jeffrey Dean, Luiz André Barroso This paper discusses the phenomenon of long latency tails in large-scale services and how to mitigate their effects. [https://research.google/pubs/pub40801/ Link]
15 The End-to-End Argument in System Design Jerome H. Saltzer, David P. Reed, David D. Clark A seminal paper that introduces the end-to-end argument, a principle in system design that helps in deciding where to place functions in a networked system. [https://web.mit.edu/Saltzer/www/publications/endtoend/endtoend.pdf Link]
16 FAWN: A Fast Array of Wimpy Nodes David G. Andersen et al. FAWN presents an architecture that uses a cluster of low-power nodes to provide efficient, scalable, and reliable storage. [https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~dga/papers/fawn-sosp09.pdf Link]
17 The Zebra Copying Garbage Collector Bill McCloskey et al. This paper discusses the Zebra garbage collector, which provides a copying collector optimized for high throughput and low pause times. [https://research.fb.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/11/the-zebra-copying-garbage-collector.pdf Link]
18 The Datacenter as a Computer: An Introduction to the Design of Warehouse-Scale Machines Luiz André Barroso, Urs Hölzle This paper introduces the concept of warehouse-scale computing and discusses the design of datacenters that function as single massive computers. [https://research.google/pubs/archive/281200.pdf Link]
19 Pregel: A System for Large-Scale Graph Processing Grzegorz Malewicz et al. Pregel is a system designed by Google for processing large-scale graphs efficiently using a vertex-centric model. [https://research.google/pubs/archive/36721.pdf Link]
20 SEDA: An Architecture for Well-Conditioned, Scalable Internet Services Matt Welsh et al. This paper presents SEDA (Staged Event-Driven Architecture), a framework for building scalable and well-conditioned internet services. [https://people.eecs.berkeley.edu/~dawnsong/teaching/spring08/Papers/seda-sosp01.pdf Link]
21 TreadMarks: Distributed Shared Memory on Standard Workstations and Operating Systems Pete Keleher et al. 22 The SWIM Gossip Protocol I. Gupta et al. This paper describes the SWIM protocol, a scalable, weakly-consistent, infection-style process group membership protocol. [https://cs.brown.edu/~irina/papers/swim.pdf Link]
23 CORFU: A Shared Log Design for Flash Clusters Dahlia Malkhi et al. CORFU introduces a scalable shared log design that leverages the properties of flash storage to provide high throughput and low latency. [https://research.cs.wisc.edu/wind/Publications/corfusosp2012.pdf Link]
