Credits: The rules for a new entrepreneurship.

# Rule Explanation
1 Learning from mistakes is overrated Focus on learning from successes to understand what works and replicate it.
2 Planning is guessing Plans can be restrictive; embrace improvisation and adaptability.
3 Make a dent in the universe Aim to make a meaningful impact with your work.
4 Scratch your own itch Create products or services that you personally want to use.
5 Draw a line in the sand Have a clear point of view to attract dedicated fans.
6 Build half a product, not a half-assed product Focus on completing fewer, high-quality projects.
7 Start at the epicenter Begin with the most essential part of your project.
8 Ignore the details early on Avoid getting bogged down by minor details at the start.
9 Making the call is making progress Make decisions to build momentum and progress.
10 Be a curator Simplify and focus on what's truly essential.
11 Sell your by-products Identify and capitalize on by-products of your work.
12 Launch now Release your product sooner than you might feel comfortable.
13 Reasons to quit Ensure your work is meaningful and impactful.
14 Meetings are toxic Keep meetings efficient and focused on real issues.
15 Good enough is fine Prioritize progress over perfection.
16 Long lists don't get done Keep to-do lists short and prioritize visually.
17 Don't copy Understand the underlying reasons for success rather than imitating.
18 Say no by default Avoid overcommitting to ideas and projects.
19 Don't confuse enthusiasm with priority Evaluate ideas based on their true worth, not initial excitement.
20 Don't write it down Focus on recurring customer requests rather than documenting every suggestion.
21 Emulate Chefs Share your processes and insights with others.
22 Go behind the scenes Offer transparency into how your business operates.
23 Press releases are spam Stand out by being personal and remarkable in your communication.
24 Drug dealers get it right Offer a free sample to attract customers.
25 Marketing is not a department It's the sum of all your actions and interactions.
26 The myth of the overnight sensation Success often requires years of hard work.
27 Do it yourself first Understand a job before hiring someone to do it.
28 Hire when it hurts Only hire when absolutely necessary.
29 Hire great writers Clear writing reflects clear thinking and communication.
30 Test drive employees Evaluate potential hires through short-term projects.
31 Take a deep breath Allow time for adjustments after changes.
32 You don't create a culture Culture is a byproduct of consistent behavior.
33 Decisions are temporary Focus on current needs and adjust as necessary.
34 Skip the rock stars Create an environment that fosters great work from everyone.
35 Don't scar on the first cut Avoid overreacting with policies to isolated incidents.
36 Inspiration is perishable Act on inspiration quickly before it fades.
