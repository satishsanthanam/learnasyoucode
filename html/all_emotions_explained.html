Credits: All emotions explained

Emotion Categories

Positive Emotions:

  • Joyful Emotions: Joy, Excitement, Elation, Amusement, Contentment, Gratitude, Hope, Optimism, Wonder, Awe, Relief, Satisfaction, Anticipation, Curiosity, Love, Pride, Resilience, Trust.
  • Love-related Emotions: Love, Compassion, Empathy, Sympathy.

Negative Emotions:

  • Sad and Depressive Emotions: Sadness, Melancholy, Despair, Grief, Loneliness, Disappointment, Yearning.
  • Angry and Aggressive Emotions: Anger, Hate, Contempt, Resentment, Frustration, Jealousy.
  • Anxious and Fearful Emotions: Fear, Anxiety, Guilt, Shame, Embarrassment, Distrust.
  • Other Negative Emotions: Disgust, Boredom, Envy, Regret, Pessimism, Confusion, Nostalgia.

Neutral Emotions:

  • Surprise
# Emotion Description Compensating-Emotions
1 Joy A feeling of happiness, delight, or pleasure often accompanied by a sense of satisfaction. Sadness
2 Sadness A state of feeling unhappy, sorrowful, or melancholic. Joy
3 Anger An intense emotional response characterized by feelings of irritation, hostility, or frustration. Calmness
4 Fear An emotional response triggered by perceived danger, threat, or harm. Courage
5 Surprise A sudden and unexpected emotional reaction to an event or stimulus. Expectation
6 Disgust A strong aversion or revulsion towards something unpleasant, offensive, or repulsive. Attraction
7 Love A deep and intense emotional attachment, affection, or fondness towards someone or something. Hate
8 Hate A strong feeling of intense dislike, hostility, or animosity towards someone or something. Love
9 Envy A feeling of discontent or resentment towards someone else's success, possessions, or qualities. Contentment
10 Jealousy A complex emotion involving feelings of insecurity, possessiveness, or fear of losing someone's affection. Trust
11 Excitement A state of heightened arousal or enthusiasm, often in anticipation of something enjoyable. Boredom
12 Anxiety A feeling of apprehension, unease, or worry about future events or uncertainties. Calmness
13 Guilt A sense of remorse, regret, or responsibility for having done something wrong. Innocence
14 Shame A painful feeling of humiliation, embarrassment, or disgrace resulting from one's own actions. Pride
15 Pride A sense of satisfaction, dignity, or self-respect derived from one's achievements. Shame
16 Contentment A state of satisfaction, fulfillment, or peace of mind with one's current circumstances. Discontentment
17 Compassion A sympathetic concern for the suffering or distress of others. Indifference
18 Empathy The ability to understand and share the feelings, thoughts, and experiences of others. Apathy
19 Sympathy A feeling of compassion or concern for someone else's situation. Indifference
20 Boredom A state of weariness, restlessness, or lack of interest in one's surroundings. Excitement
21 Loneliness A feeling of sadness or isolation resulting from a perceived lack of meaningful connections. Companionship
22 Gratitude A feeling of thankfulness or appreciation towards someone or something. Ingratitude
23 Regret A sense of disappointment or remorse over past actions. Satisfaction
24 Hope A positive emotional outlook or expectation for the future. Despair
25 Despair A state of utter hopelessness, helplessness, or deep sadness. Hope
26 Frustration A feeling of annoyance, disappointment, or agitation resulting from obstacles. Satisfaction
27 Relief A sense of reassurance, comfort, or release from stress after a challenging situation. Anxiety
28 Satisfaction A feeling of contentment, gratification, or fulfillment resulting from achieving one's goals. Frustration
29 Anticipation A feeling of excitement or eagerness about future events. Surprise
30 Curiosity A strong desire or interest to explore, learn, or understand something new. Indifference
31 Confusion A state of uncertainty, perplexity, or disorientation resulting from a lack of understanding. Clarity
32 Disappointment A feeling of dissatisfaction or letdown resulting from unfulfilled expectations. Satisfaction
33 Embarrassment A feeling of self-consciousness, awkwardness, or discomfort resulting from social scrutiny. Confidence
34 Elation A state of extreme happiness, excitement, or euphoria. Melancholy
35 Melancholy A deep, pensive, or introspective sadness often accompanied by a sense of longing. Elation
36 Awe An overwhelming sense of wonder, reverence, or admiration inspired by something extraordinary. Disgust
37 Trust A confident reliance or belief in the reliability, integrity, or goodwill of someone. Distrust
38 Distrust A lack of confidence, suspicion, or skepticism towards someone. Trust
39 Amusement A feeling of enjoyment, humor, or entertainment resulting from something funny. Contempt
40 Contempt A feeling of disdain, scorn, or disrespect towards someone considered inferior. Respect
41 Resentment A lingering feeling of bitterness, indignation, or anger towards someone for perceived wrongs. Forgiveness
42 Nostalgia A sentimental longing or affectionate remembrance for past experiences. Present-focus
43 Wonder A feeling of astonishment, marvel, or fascination inspired by something extraordinary. Indifference
44 Resilience A capacity to adapt, bounce back, or recover from adversity. Vulnerability
45 Grief A deep and profound sorrow or mourning experienced in response to loss. Acceptance
46 Optimism A positive outlook or belief in the likelihood of favorable outcomes. Pessimism
47 Pessimism A negative or cynical outlook characterized by an expectation of unfavorable outcomes. Optimism
48 Yearning A strong, intense longing or desire for something that is absent or unattainable. Contentment
