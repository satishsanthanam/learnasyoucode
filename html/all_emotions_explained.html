Credits: All emotions explained

Emotion Categories

Positive Emotions:

  • Joyful Emotions: Joy, Excitement, Elation, Amusement, Contentment, Gratitude, Hope, Optimism, Wonder, Awe, Relief, Satisfaction, Anticipation, Curiosity, Love, Pride, Resilience, Trust.
  • Love-related Emotions: Love, Compassion, Empathy, Sympathy.

Negative Emotions:

  • Sad and Depressive Emotions: Sadness, Melancholy, Despair, Grief, Loneliness, Disappointment, Yearning.
  • Angry and Aggressive Emotions: Anger, Hate, Contempt, Resentment, Frustration, Jealousy.
  • Anxious and Fearful Emotions: Fear, Anxiety, Guilt, Shame, Embarrassment, Distrust.
  • Other Negative Emotions: Disgust, Boredom, Envy, Regret, Pessimism, Confusion, Nostalgia.

Neutral Emotions:

  • Surprise
Emotion Description
Joy A feeling of happiness, delight, or pleasure often accompanied by a sense of satisfaction. Joy can arise from positive experiences, accomplishments, or connections with others.
Sadness A state of feeling unhappy, sorrowful, or melancholic. Sadness can result from loss, disappointment, or unmet expectations.
Anger An intense emotional response characterized by feelings of irritation, hostility, or frustration. Anger can arise from perceived threats, injustices, or conflicts.
Fear An emotional response triggered by perceived danger, threat, or harm. Fear can manifest as anxiety, nervousness, or a desire to avoid the source of fear.
Surprise A sudden and unexpected emotional reaction to an event or stimulus. Surprise can be positive or negative and may involve feelings of astonishment, disbelief, or confusion.
Disgust A strong aversion or revulsion towards something unpleasant, offensive, or repulsive. Disgust can be triggered by sensory experiences, moral violations, or social norms.
Love A deep and intense emotional attachment, affection, or fondness towards someone or something. Love can encompass various forms such as romantic love, familial love, or platonic love.
Hate A strong feeling of intense dislike, hostility, or animosity towards someone or something. Hate can be fueled by anger, resentment, or prejudice.
Envy A feeling of discontent or resentment towards someone else's success, possessions, or qualities, often accompanied by a desire to possess what they have.
Jealousy A complex emotion involving feelings of insecurity, possessiveness, or fear of losing someone's affection or attention to a rival.
Excitement A state of heightened arousal or enthusiasm, often in anticipation of something enjoyable or stimulating.
Anxiety A feeling of apprehension, unease, or worry about future events or uncertainties. Anxiety can manifest as physical symptoms such as racing heart, sweating, or trembling.
Guilt A sense of remorse, regret, or responsibility for having done something wrong or causing harm to others.
Shame A painful feeling of humiliation, embarrassment, or disgrace resulting from one's own actions, behaviors, or perceived shortcomings.
Pride A sense of satisfaction, dignity, or self-respect derived from one's achievements, qualities, or identity.
Contentment A state of satisfaction, fulfillment, or peace of mind with one's current circumstances or possessions.
Compassion A sympathetic concern for the suffering or distress of others, often accompanied by a desire to alleviate their pain or help them in some way.
Empathy The ability to understand and share the feelings, thoughts, and experiences of others, often leading to a sense of connection and solidarity.
Sympathy A feeling of compassion or concern for someone else's situation without necessarily experiencing their emotions as one's own.
Boredom A state of weariness, restlessness, or lack of interest in one's surroundings or activities, often resulting from monotony or repetition.
Loneliness A feeling of sadness or isolation resulting from a perceived lack of meaningful connections or companionship with others.
Gratitude A feeling of thankfulness or appreciation towards someone or something for their kindness, generosity, or positive impact on one's life.
Regret A sense of disappointment or remorse over past actions, choices, or missed opportunities.
Hope A positive emotional outlook or expectation for the future, often accompanied by a belief in the possibility of better circumstances or outcomes.
Despair A state of utter hopelessness, helplessness, or deep sadness, often resulting from a sense of loss or failure.
Frustration A feeling of annoyance, disappointment, or agitation resulting from obstacles, setbacks, or unmet expectations.
Relief A sense of reassurance, comfort, or release from stress, anxiety, or tension after a challenging or difficult situation.
Satisfaction A feeling of contentment, gratification, or fulfillment resulting from achieving one's goals, desires, or expectations.
Anticipation A feeling of excitement or eagerness about future events, possibilities, or outcomes.
Curiosity A strong desire or interest to explore, learn, or understand something new or unknown.
Confusion A state of uncertainty, perplexity, or disorientation resulting from a lack of understanding or clarity.
Disappointment A feeling of dissatisfaction or letdown resulting from unfulfilled expectations, hopes, or desires.
Embarrassment A feeling of self-consciousness, awkwardness, or discomfort resulting from social scrutiny or a perceived failure to meet expectations.
Elation A state of extreme happiness, excitement, or euphoria, often resulting from a sense of achievement or success.
Melancholy A deep, pensive, or introspective sadness often accompanied by a sense of longing or nostalgia.
Awe An overwhelming sense of wonder, reverence, or admiration inspired by something extraordinary, grand, or sublime.
Trust A confident reliance or belief in the reliability, integrity, or goodwill of someone or something.
Distrust A lack of confidence, suspicion, or skepticism towards someone or something, often due to perceived dishonesty or unreliability.
Amusement A feeling of enjoyment, humor, or entertainment resulting from something funny, amusing, or entertaining.
Contempt A feeling of disdain, scorn, or disrespect towards someone or something considered inferior, unworthy, or despicable.
Resentment A lingering feeling of bitterness, indignation, or anger towards someone for perceived wrongs or injustices.
Nostalgia A sentimental longing or affectionate remembrance for past experiences, places, or relationships.
Wonder A feeling of astonishment, marvel, or fascination inspired by something extraordinary, mysterious, or beautiful.
Resilience A capacity to adapt, bounce back, or recover from adversity, setbacks, or challenges.
Grief A deep and profound sorrow or mourning experienced in response to loss, particularly the death of a loved one.
Optimism A positive outlook or belief in the likelihood of favorable outcomes or future success.
Pessimism A negative or cynical outlook characterized by an expectation of unfavorable outcomes or future failure.
Yearning A strong, intense longing or desire for something that is absent or unattainable.
